Publication Ethics Statement

Our Commitment to Academic Integrity

Journal of Advances in Management, Engineering and Science (JAMES) is committed to upholding the highest ethical standards in academic publishing. JAMES adheres to internationally recognized principles and best practices in publication ethics, ensuring the integrity, originality, and quality of all published research.

JAMES promotes the publication of original scholarly materials across all fields of management, engineering, science, and their interdisciplinary applications. The journal provides clear guidelines to authors, reviewers, and editors to maintain transparency, accountability, and integrity throughout the publication process.

Authorship and Contributorship

All authors listed on a manuscript submitted to JAMES must have:

  • Made substantial contributions to the conception, design, data collection, analysis, or interpretation of the research.
  • Drafted or critically revised the manuscript for intellectual content.
  • Approved the final version of the manuscript for publication.
  • Agreed to be accountable for all aspects of the work.

Acknowledgment of individuals who contributed but do not meet authorship criteria can be included in the acknowledgments section.

Use of AI/LLM/AI-Assisted Technologies in Authorship

Permitted Uses

Limited use of AI tools to support writing and data analysis is permitted; however, authors are responsible for reviewing and editing AI-generated content. Disclosure of AI tool usage in the manuscript is required.

Restrictions

AI tools cannot be credited as authors. The use of AI-generated images or videos is strictly prohibited.

Academic Peer Review

All submissions to JAMES undergo a rigorous double-anonymized peer review process to ensure unbiased evaluation and high scholarly standards. Reviewers are expected to provide detailed and constructive feedback to support the improvement of submitted manuscripts.

AI Use in Peer Review

Use of AI in Peer Review is strictly prohibited to maintain confidentiality and avoid bias.

Research Ethics

All research published in JAMES must comply with relevant ethical standards:

Human Subjects

Studies must have prior ethical approval and informed consent. Clinical trials must be registered.

Animal Research

Studies must follow ethical guidelines for care, handling, and experimentation.

Plant, Paleontological, Geological, and Heritage Research

Studies must comply with institutional, national, and international guidelines, including appropriate permits and documentation.

Informed Consent and Participant Privacy

Authors must ensure participants provide informed consent and understand the use of their data. Identifiable information should only be included with explicit consent.

Copyright, Intellectual Property, and Plagiarism

Authors must ensure originality and obtain permissions for third-party materials. JAMES follows strict copyright and plagiarism policies.

Plagiarism Policy

Submissions containing plagiarized content will be rejected, and authors may be barred from future submissions. Proper citation and acknowledgment of previous work are mandatory.

Simultaneous Submission (Self-Plagiarism)

Manuscripts submitted to JAMES must be original and not under review, published, or accepted elsewhere. Prior work must be properly cited and attributed.

Important Notice

Failure to disclose prior publications may be considered unethical and lead to rejection or further investigation.

Our Commitment

JAMES is committed to fostering transparency, accountability, and integrity in academic publishing and to maintaining a fair, rigorous, and ethical review and publication process for all contributors.
